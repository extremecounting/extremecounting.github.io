Riots in Chicago!

Author: Jacob Hatch

September 1st, 1968

A few months ago, Hubert Humphrey became the primary candidate for the democratic party. Hubert Humphrey, the former vice president of Lyndon Johnson, was disliked by the antiwar people. Humphrey seems to have strong support from people who like the war.

Things heated up, when 10,000 protesters came to persuade the democratic party into picking a more antiwar candidate. According to Chicago's mayor, Richard J Daley, "as long as I am mayor... there will be law and order." Delay mobilzied over 5,000 National Guard members, and 12,000 police officers.

Nevertheless, when protesters assembled in a downtown park with intent to march to the convection. The whole world watched, as the Chicago police came and beat them with nightsticks and sprayed them with mace. Many people tried to flee or fight back, as they were getting attacked. People started to riot.

In the convention hall, they were debating over an antiwar plank, when they heard of the nearby riot going on and what happened. When delegates heard of this, they started shouting at Daley, who was a delegate himself. The whole world was also watching the convention hall on their TV, and now everyone sees the disorder the democratic party is capable of.
