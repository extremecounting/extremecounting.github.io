Civilian Massacre?

Author: Jacob Hatch

March 20th, 1969

On March 16th, an American platoon commanded by Lieutenant William Calley, Jr. murdered many innocent villagers. The villgers were in a small town in the Northern part of South Vietnam, called My Lai. Calley's group was in the area to look around for any Vietcong members that they could find.

William Calley was reportedly looking for Vietcong members in My Lai, and couldn't find any. Because of this, he had the soldiers round up the villagers. Once soldiers rounded up all the villagers and huddled them all into a group, the Lieutenant ordered them to "Kill anything that breathed", and they opened fire on the group of innocent Southern Vietnamese civilians. One man recalls that he "poured about four clips into the group"

Do you think the soldiers under Lieutenant William Calley, Jr. should be held responsible? The troops all insisted that they weren't responsible because they were just following the Lieutenant's orders, do you agree? 25 officers were charged with some responsiblility for the event, but William Calley was the only one to be convicted and imprisoned.
