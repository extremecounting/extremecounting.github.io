Our troops come home!

Author: Jacob Hatch

March 29th, 1973

Nixon delayed in his promise of peace, and made a "final push" against North Vietnam. The Thiue regime rejected Henry Kissinger's plan to because Northern Vietnamese troops stationed in South Vietnam alarmed them. Chances of a deal started to look very slim, so Nixon made one last push against North Vietnam. American planes dropped over 100,000 bombs over the two biggest cities in North Vietnam, Haiphong and Hanoi around Christmas. Finally, everyone seemed to be done with war, both America and the enemies.

On January 27th, America signed a "Agreement on Ending the War and Restoring Peace in Vietnam". In this deal, troops from North Vietnam would stay in South Vietnam, but Nixon promised that he'd react "with full force" if North Vietnam broke the agreement. I don't know if North Vietnam is to be trusted, but it appears that we are finally withdrawing from the war, whether for the better or the worse.

Because of this, American troops started started heading back home. Today, March 29th, the last of the American troops withdrew from Vietnam and headed home. We are officially no longer part of the Vietnam War! Most people seem to be in favor of this, as this is offically the longest war in U.S. history.
